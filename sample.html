Trek to PlumDhara Waterfall

There is a wonderful waterfall at sissu which is around 1.5 Km from Sangam Camps. This is undoubtedly the best and most beautiful waterfall in entire north India approx height of which is around 50 meters, but is still nearly unexplored. Reason is not many people visit these areas and those who visit have little information about its existence. The obstacle to those who like venture out is that from the road the fall is not seen propely (the base is not seen) and looks just like many other fall. U need to go closer to relish its beauty. Its really impressive!!! One can walk unto the base of the fall, which will take 15-20 mins from the highway. You can also climb up to the place where the water actually strikes the ground, as the climb is not very tough.

Those with lil more ethu and stamina could even climb to the top. One shepherd climbed to the top with around 20 kgs of luggage in no time!!!! So next time u r in that region do visit this wonderful waterfall.

Trek to Aliyas Lake

This lake is named has many variants of its name. Some call it Ghepan Ghat, GSI people call it Gepang Gath, gaddis’ call it Ghepan Ka Alyas. So who is Ghepan? Ghepan is the most revered deity of Lahaul Valley. So much so that he is called Raja Ghepan, the King. A peak by the same name rises above the Sissu village that stands tall like an Emperor. A solitary tower keeping a constant vigil. A massive rock face guarding the valley of Lahaul.

The trek starts from Labrang Gompa, a two kilometer detour from Sissu. The easiest way is to follow the ridge that starts from the end point of Labrang Gompa road. Trek is of moderate difficulty and barring few patches here and there, it’s all smooth sailing of 14.1 km from Labrang Gompa.

It is one of the fast retreating glaciers in Himachal Pradesh, on an average the glacier has receded 44.98 m/year during 2003-2012. The area of this lake has increased from 0.26 sq. km. in 1965 to 0.78 sq. km. in 2012. Most importantly, the lake has been determined theoretically stable and the earlier fears of imminent catastrophe were plain exaggeration.

Location Get to know us

Sangam Camps is located at Sissu, beautiful valley of Lahoul surrounded by Mountains and situated at bank of two rivers that meets (Sangam). Sangam Camps is open for 6 months due to snow cover during the winter from October to mid April. We have at present 10 Tents with attached Bathroom and common dinning hall.

Sissu (also known as Khagling) is a small town in the Lahoul valley of Himachal Pradesh in India. Gyephang peak is visible from this village. It is around 85 K.M from Manali and located on the right bank of Chandra River.

When the rest of India is reeling under extensive heat of summers or heavy rainfall during the monsoon and you have no other place in mind for your holidays, Lahoul is the perfect destination. Located at an altitude of 10235 ft above sea level, bluest of skies, beautiful lake, Water Fall, Lahoul is the perfect getaway from the oppressive heat of the plains. Also known for its unique culture, monasteries and people is becoming one of the hottest destinations of India. All this and more makes your visit to Lahoul more than just a leisure travel
